"Why you and I relate to a middle-aged depressed horse named Bojack" is book that I made using content sourced from the internet to answer the question it is named after. In this book, I juxtapose both informative content and the script of Bojack horseman in which I highlight lines said by the character Bojack that reflect his temperement. The cover, "So a horse walks into your TV" is suppose to hint to the contents of the book by refering to the topic being a TV show and it's comedic nature.
This series of posters was produced with the intent of promoting the final critique of one of my classes. I made these posters as a series of three so it would catch more attention since the medium was intended to be posted on wall with various other advertising. For the same effect, I also decided to have the third posster be dissplayed loosely in the hopes that the movement would make it stand out among other still elements. In the design I used my own font, negative, which I talk further in depth about below.
I created the font negative by exploring the negative space around letterforms to create a font that verges between the readible and the abstract. For this purpose I made this font monowidth and monoheight to decrease the differences between it's letters. It's presentation pamphlet also plays with the asspects of readability by incorporatiing a find the word game with words that define it like "readability","abstract" and so on.
OSFA(one size fits all) is a brand that strives to create a true one size fits all by inccorporating ideas of adjustability into it's designs. OSFA empowers the modern woman with a capsule wardrobe inspired by workwear and adapted to the everyday life. It's promotional videos(a longer one intended for presentation and a shorter one intended for social media promotions ) and it's logo reflect the brand's New York roots through the use of a custom font and the iconic soho streets. (A project in collaboration with Mathis Ekkebus, Eileen Chen and Soobin Hong)

Both artists used performance to overwhelm their viewerss in sounds so for my design of apatern I incorporated the idea of the overwhelming and omnipresent nature of TV static. Inspired by the TV show Life after people, this project documents predictions of what would happen to New York City 50 years later if people abandoned it. This project usess the idea of a plane inbetween 2D and 3D to represent both the stillness left behind if the people left and the dynamic nature of the changes that would occur. This project was intended to be presented via airdrop to perssonal smart phones to simulate our current system for flood alerts. This also creates and overwhelming sensation in a room when several people receive and play it at once highlighting the gravity of the situation.
